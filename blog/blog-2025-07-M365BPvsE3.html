Microsoft 365 Business Premium vs E3: Choosing the Right Licence in 2025 and Beyond

Published 16 July 2025 | Last updated 16 July 2025

TL;DR → Business Premium is a better fit for most Australian SMBs under 300 seats. E3 suits firms needing hybrid AD, litigation holds, or 1,000+ user scale.

Why This Matters

Microsoft 365 licensing isn’t just a box-ticking exercise anymore. With endpoint security, compliance, and remote work baked into every plan, your choice of licence has a direct impact on risk, productivity, and cost.

Key Feature Comparison

Business Premium: Full security stack (Defender, Intune), up to 300 users.
E3: No user limit, hybrid AD support, legal/compliance features.

Cost Breakdown

Business Premium: ~$33/user/month (AUD)
M365 E3: ~$53/user/month + cost for Intune/Defender if needed

Security & Compliance

  • Business Premium includes Defender for Business (with EDR, ASR)
  • E3 has Defender for Endpoint P1 but lacks automated remediation
  • E3 includes Litigation Hold, Unlimited Archive Mailbox, Hybrid Join

FAQs

Can I mix Business Premium and E3 licences?

Yes. You can assign different plans to different users based on role. Just be careful with policy scoping.

What happens if I exceed 300 users on Business Premium?

You’ll be unable to assign more BP licences. You’ll need to migrate users to E3 or contact a CSP.

Does Business Premium include Teams and OneDrive?

Yes, including 1TB OneDrive storage and full Teams features.
